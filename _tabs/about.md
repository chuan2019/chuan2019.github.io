# About Me

Hi, I'm **Chuan Zhang** — a senior AI and software engineer with a Ph.D. in Applied Mathematics. Over the past decade I've built backend systems and distributed data platforms, and recently, LLM-powered applications. I'm currently working at **[Deluxe Media](https://www.bydeluxe.com/)** in Burbank, CA, building a microservice platform on AWS that uses generative AI to automate media localization related workflows, movie subtitle machine translation evaluation and post-editing, among others.

Before joining Deluxe Media, I led an AI engineering team at **[AXA XL](https://axaxl.com/)** building an LLM-driven document intelligence platform, with early forays into knowledge-graph modeling. Earlier, I spent three and a half years at **[Activision](https://www.activision.com/)** building experimentation platforms for A/B testing, multi-armed bandit, dynamic segmentation, and KPI services -- running at game scale, with 8+ million concurrent players and 30K+ RPS. And before the game industry, I optimized SQL query engines in C/C++ at **[Teradata](https://www.teradata.com/)** and built distributed pattern databases at **[ASML](https://www.asml.com/en)**. The throughline across all of it: distributed systems and data infrastructure, and, more recently, agentic AI tooling — MCP, LangChain, LangGraph, and the like — to get LLMs to hold up in production.

I write about whatever engineering problem currently has my attention — usually AI Agents, RAG systems, Python internals, observability, or applied ML — and try to capture the architecture decisions and tradeoffs that don't always make it into papers or docs. Previously published on [Medium](https://chuan-zhang.medium.com), this blog is now my primary home for long-form technical writing.

## What I Write About

- **LLMs & RAG** — retrieval-augmented generation, knowledge graphs, reasoning pipelines, and grounding LLMs in structured data
- **Python Engineering** — async patterns, protocol types, runtime validation, and the language features that matter at production scale
- **Observability & Systems** — OpenTelemetry, Datadog, DIY stacks, and building platforms you can actually trust to tell you what's happening
- **ML & Data** — applied machine learning, deep learning, NLP, and the messy reality of building models that work in production
